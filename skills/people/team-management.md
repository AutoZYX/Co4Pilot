# Team Management Skill

## Overview

This skill helps principal investigators and research group leaders build, manage, and develop effective research teams. It covers lab culture, role assignment, conflict resolution, collaboration frameworks, meeting facilitation, and team-level productivity optimization.

## Building a Research Team

### Team Composition Strategy
- **Core team**: PhD students + postdocs forming the research backbone
- **Extended team**: Master's students, visiting scholars, undergraduate assistants
- **Collaborative ring**: Industry partners, co-PIs, international collaborators
- **Support staff**: Lab managers, technicians, administrative assistants

### Hiring and Recruitment
- Define clear role expectations before recruiting
- Use structured interviews with standardized evaluation criteria
- Assess both technical skills and cultural fit
- Consider diversity in backgrounds, skills, and perspectives
- Create onboarding checklists for new team members

### Onboarding Framework (First 90 Days)
| Phase | Timeline | Activities |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Pre-arrival | 2 weeks before | IT setup, desk/lab space, reading list, mentor assignment |
| Week 1 | Days 1-5 | Lab tour, safety training, team introductions, expectations doc |
| Month 1 | Weeks 1-4 | Shadow senior members, attend all group meetings, literature deep-dive |
| Month 2-3 | Weeks 5-12 | First independent task, regular 1-on-1s, 90-day review |

## Lab Culture and Values

### Establishing Group Norms
- **Communication**: Response time expectations, preferred channels (email vs. chat vs. in-person)
- **Collaboration**: Open-door policy, data sharing practices, co-authorship guidelines
- **Work-life balance**: Core hours, vacation expectations, mental health awareness
- **Academic integrity**: Zero tolerance for fabrication, clear citation practices
- **Inclusivity**: Respectful language, accommodation of cultural differences

### Group Meeting Structure
- **Weekly lab meeting**: 60-90 min, rotating presentations, constructive feedback
- **Journal club**: Bi-weekly, deep dive into recent papers
- **One-on-one meetings**: Weekly or bi-weekly with each team member
- **Annual retreat**: Full-day event for strategic planning and team building

### Meeting Best Practices
- Circulate agenda 24 hours in advance
- Assign timekeeper and note-taker (rotate roles)
- Start with wins and progress before addressing challenges
- End with clear action items and owners
- Archive meeting notes in shared repository

## Role Assignment and Delegation

### Matching Tasks to Team Members
- Consider skill level, career stage, and development goals
- Stretch assignments for growth (with mentoring support)
- Pair junior and senior members on complex tasks
- Avoid overloading high performers; distribute work equitably

### Delegation Framework (RACI)
- **Responsible**: Person doing the work
- **Accountable**: Person with final authority/approval
- **Consulted**: People providing input
- **Informed**: People who need to know the outcome

### Project Assignment Matrix
| Project Type | Best Assigned To | Supervision Level |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Exploratory pilot | Senior PhD / postdoc | Light touch |
| Core methodology | PhD student | Regular check-ins |
| Data collection | Master's / undergrad | Close supervision |
| Paper writing | PhD + PI | Collaborative |
| Grant admin | Lab manager | Periodic review |

## Conflict Resolution

### Common Conflicts in Research Teams
- Authorship disputes
- Resource allocation disagreements
- Personality clashes
- Workload imbalance perceptions
- Intellectual property concerns
- Communication style differences

### Resolution Approaches
1. **Prevention**: Clear policies, transparent decision-making, documented agreements
2. **Early intervention**: Address issues when small; do not let resentment build
3. **Mediation framework**: Listen to both sides separately, then jointly; focus on interests, not positions
4. **Escalation path**: PI mediation, department ombudsperson, graduate school resources
5. **Documentation**: Record agreements and follow-up actions

## Collaboration Frameworks

### Internal Collaboration
- Shared code repositories with clear contribution guidelines
- Common data storage with access controls
- Collaborative writing platforms (Overleaf, Google Docs)
- Regular knowledge-sharing sessions (brown bag lunches, skill workshops)

### External Collaboration
- Define roles, responsibilities, and IP ownership upfront
- Use collaboration agreements for multi-institution projects
- Establish communication cadence (monthly calls, quarterly reviews)
- Plan co-authorship early; revisit as contributions evolve

## Team Productivity

### Tracking Group Progress
- Maintain a shared project dashboard (Notion, Trello, or spreadsheet)
- Quarterly goal setting with OKR framework
- Annual individual development plans aligned with group goals
- Publication pipeline tracker: ideas, drafts, under review, published

### Productivity Patterns
- Protect deep work time: designate "no meeting" blocks
- Batch administrative tasks
- Use templates for recurring documents
- Automate data collection and analysis pipelines where possible

## Claude Prompt Templates

### Team Assessment
```
I lead a research team with [N] members: [list roles and levels].
Our current challenges include: [describe issues].
Please help me:
1. Assess team dynamics and identify potential improvements
2. Suggest role adjustments or task redistribution
3. Create an action plan for the next quarter
```

### Conflict Mediation Prep
```
Two team members have a disagreement about [topic].
Person A's perspective: [summary]
Person B's perspective: [summary]
Help me prepare for a mediation meeting:
1. Key questions to ask each party
2. Potential common ground
3. Fair resolution options
4. Follow-up plan
```

### Group Meeting Planning
```
I need to plan our weekly lab meeting for [date].
Updates expected from: [list members and their projects]
Special topics to discuss: [list]
Please create:
1. A structured agenda with time allocations
2. Discussion prompts for the special topics
3. A template for action items
```

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Team roles and expectations are documented and shared
- [ ] Regular 1-on-1 meetings are scheduled with each member
- [ ] Group norms are established and revisited annually
- [ ] Authorship and IP policies are clearly documented
- [ ] Onboarding process is standardized
- [ ] Conflict resolution procedures are known to all members
- [ ] Project progress is tracked transparently
- [ ] Individual development plans exist for each team member
- [ ] Work-life balance is actively supported and modeled
- [ ] External collaborations have clear agreements
